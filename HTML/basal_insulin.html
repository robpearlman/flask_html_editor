• Used to control hepatic glucose output and prevent ketosis.
  • Provides background insulin action over 24 hours, independent of carbohydrate intake. 
  • Lantus and Levemir are long acting insulin analogues, with slow onset of action: around 1-2 hours, minimal or no peak, and offset by 12-24 hrs. Lantus is usually given once daily, usually at night. Levemir is usually given bd.
  • Protaphane or Humulin NPH are intermediate acting insulins with shorter half life than Lantus or Levemir);  a peak at 4-6 hours (which may be useful to control hyperglycaemia due to glucocorticoids); and offset by 8-14 hr.
