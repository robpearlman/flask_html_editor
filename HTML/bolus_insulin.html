• Used to prevent BGL rise with a meal and to correct hyperglycaemia under supplemental dosing. 
  • Bolus insulins that are commonly used with meals and as supplemental insulin are Humalog, Novorapid, Apidra
  • Characteristics of these insulins are rapid onset within 15 min, a rapid peak at around 1hr, and rapid offset by 4hrs.
  • When bolus insulin is used to cover for carbohydrate intake, it is given with the meal. 
  • When bolus insulin is used under supplemental dosing, It is given either with the meal when patient is eating (added to the dose required to cover for carbohydrate intake) or q4h when NBM. 
  • The total amount of supplemental insulin in a prior time period can be used to guide any required adjustment to scheduled insulin dosages (Link to Scheduled Insulin Charting).
