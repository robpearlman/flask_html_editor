• Programmable device infusing insulin subcutaneously, used mainly by people with T1DM
  • All insulin pump parameters should be recorded either on a standard local (hospital/LHD) chart or in the hospital notes and any doses given by the patient should be sighted and recorded
  • If the pump fails or patient is not competent to self manage the pump during the inpatient stay, the regime should be changed to a basal bolus SC insulin regimen (or IV insulin where appropriate)
  • All pumps use only rapid acting insulin (Humalog, Novorapid, Apidra) – so there is no depot of insulin. Ketosis will arise after 3 hours if insulin supply from pump is interrupted without provision of alternate supply of insulin.
