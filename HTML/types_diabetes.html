T1DM
  • Diabetes due to absolute insulin deficiency due to autoimmune beta cell destruction
  • At risk of ketosis if insulin stopped or insulin requirements increased
  • Must have insulin continued, even when NBM
  • Always involve endocrinology team or senior HCP with expertise in management of T1DM
T2DM
  • Diabetes due to variable combination of insulin deficiency and insulin resistance
  • When on insulin, this should be continued in hospital, even when NBM
  • These patients are at risk of metabolic decompensation when a severe illness is the cause for hospital admission, or where their condition is predominantly due to an insulin secretory defect – eg long standing T2DM requiring basal-bolus insulin at home
Pancreatic insufficiency related diabetes

Examples include patients who undergo partial or total pancreatectomy, those with cystic fibrosis related diabetes, those with chronic pancreatitis or a history of excess alcohol ingestion

Steroid induced diabetes

Diabetes arising in those taking glucocorticoid medications to treat a variety of conditions
